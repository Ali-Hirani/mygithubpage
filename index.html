Livingston International - IT

The story of my first real work experience begins in Fall of 2013, I was a first-year student with no prior job experience and I felt in over my head. I did however have a genuine knack for troubleshooting and I was always the go-to guy in my family for any PC related issues; I also happen to have built my own PC a few years prior. I ended up using these as talking points in my six IT interviews and landed a job at Livingston International as PC Support Specialist. That job title was misleading since I ended up supporting WYSE terminals, BlackBerry's, MFUs and a dozen other things. All in all it was an amazing experience, and the company had already been hiring coop students from the university for years. I soon found out that most of these past coop students were upper year students, and that despite my abilities in personal troubleshooting, this job wasn't very straightforward (see resume for the type of work I did). The first few weeks was non-stop learning and applying, I was the new guy. By the end, I was managing the ticket queue as team captain, a privilege normally give to coops for a single week towards the end of the term. I felt so confident in holding the role that I requested an extension on my week to a month. During that time, the ever consistent flow of tickets was drastically mitigated and the queue went from 250 tickets to 150 on my watch. Many of these tickets were wrongly assigned in the first place and it was largely a matter of someone taking the initiative to go through each ticket and reassign and close them as needed. That someone was me. It was then I realized that the single most important thing I needed to do for my future co-op jobs was to make an impact and leave my mark.
